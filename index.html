Multiple Intelligences

Explore the incredible variety of skills and talents that make up human intelligence, according to Howard Gardner's theory.

Linguistic

The ability to use language effectively, whether orally or in writing, to express oneself or to understand others. It is manifested in writers, poets, and public speakers.

Logical-Mathematical

The ability to reason logically, solve mathematical problems, and think abstractly. It is the intelligence of scientists, engineers, and programmers.

Spatial

The ability to perceive the visual and spatial world accurately, and to transform those perceptions. It is common in architects, artists, and navigators.

Musical

Sensitivity to rhythm, pitch, melody, and timbre. Those with this intelligence excel as musicians, composers, and singers.

Bodily-Kinesthetic

The ability to use one's body skillfully and in a controlled manner. It is the intelligence of athletes, dancers, and craftspeople.

Intrapersonal

The ability to understand oneself, including feelings, goals, and motivations. It is manifested in philosophers, psychologists, and spiritual leaders.

Interpersonal

The ability to understand and interact effectively with other people. It is the intelligence of educators, salespeople, and leaders.

Naturalist

The ability to recognize and classify species in nature. It is the intelligence of biologists, botanists, and ecologists.
